ERM, Quality Assurance, and risk mitigation with effective controls

Foundations for transformation.

There are 34 Agencies with their own subordinate Organizations within DOD facing a complex landscape of operational and financial challenges. We recognize these vulnerabilities in internal controls, evidenced by deficiencies and audit findings. These weaknesses threatened the DOD finance, accounting, and disbursement processes. A lack of standardized quality assurance and inconsistent understanding of risk management principles created potential for errors, compliance issues, and impacted the ability to effectively manage resources.

The multi-pronged approach:

  • Targeted Corrective Action: Close collaboration with Managers to develop and implement tailored corrective action plans that address, and resolve identified SOC 1 and SOC 2 deficiencies.
  • Quality Assurance Excellence: Establishing clear standards and processes to elevate the quality of finance, accounting, and disbursement operations with an annual Quality Assurance Programs (QAPs).
  • Empowering Through Education: Investing in Staff development on fundamental internal control concepts and risk management principles to foster a culture of awareness and proactive problem-solving.
  • Root Cause Analysis and Solutions: Critical thinking and analytical skills that identify the real problems for operational issues so targeted solutions can be developed and implemented.
  • Streamlining Through Policy: Revising ERM procedures and policies for enhanced compliance, operational continuity, and efficiency.
  • Strategic Audit Analysis: Meticulous support of ERM activities by reviewing internal control-related findings and recommendations from audits to strategically map risks for internal control failures to develop plans of activities and milestones (POAMs).
  • Documentation Rigor: Talent in identifying and reviewing key supporting documentation, identifying weaknesses, gaps, and deficiencies to strengthen Organizational control environments.
  • Validating Controls: Development and follow through of robust plans for randomly testing Organization's internal controls to identify deficiencies and provide actionable recommendations for improvement.

  • Risk Mitigation: Educating employees on risk management principles and analyzing audit findings enabled us to proactively identify and mitigate potential risks, safeguarding our resources and reputation.
  • Optimized Resource Utilization: By enhancing efficiency and reducing errors, we maximized the use of our resources, ensuring that funding was used effectively and strategically.
  • Positive Impact on Funding: The overall improvement in operational efficiency and risk management that positively changed our ability to effectively manage and secure funding, demonstrating our commitment to responsible stewardship.
